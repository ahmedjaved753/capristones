# 2026-05-06 — Brand-story section on Home ("From Quarry to Home")

You provided two paragraphs of brand copy and asked for the best place to add them. They've been added to the **homepage**, in a new section between the opening hero and the "Our Collections" preview — that's the natural seam for an elevator-pitch about who Capri Stone is, before visitors start browsing materials.

## What changed

A new section now sits below the hero, on a clean white background, with the headline:

> **From Quarry _to Home_**

(The same editorial pattern used elsewhere on the site — terracotta first phrase, warm-sienna italic second phrase.) The eyebrow above it reads **WHO WE ARE**.

The two paragraphs of your copy appear next to the headline:

> At Capri Stone, innovation, creativity, and sustainability guide everything we do — delivering excellence from the quarry to your home.
>
> We import premium natural stone, quartz, and kitchen cabinetry, offering carefully curated materials to elevate both residential and commercial spaces with timeless beauty and quality.

The section uses the same grid + animation rhythm as the existing "Our Collections" and "Walk-Ins Welcome" sections, so it reads as a natural part of the homepage rather than a bolted-on block. A subtle horizontal divider separates it from the collections section below.

## Why this placement

Three options were considered:

1. **A new "About" page.** Rejected — the site's confirmed scope is Home / Natural Stone / Quartz / Contact (with Cabinets and Shower Panels shadowed by Coming Soon). Adding a route just for two paragraphs is heavier than the content warrants, and it would mean visitors have to click an extra link to read the brand statement.
2. **The Contact page.** Rejected — Contact is purpose-built for visiting the showroom (phone, email, address, hours). Mixing brand mission into it dilutes that page's job.
3. **The homepage, between the hero and the collections grid.** Chosen — this is the "elevator pitch" position. The hero gives the visual hook; this new section explains who Capri Stone is and what they do; then the collections preview lets visitors browse. It's the same flow most premium-brand websites use.

## A small naming note

Your copy uses the brand name **Capri Stone**. The site's navigation already says "Capri Stone" (top-left wordmark), and the email domain on file is `@capristones.com`. The footer and browser-tab title still say "Premium Stones" — that mismatch existed before this round and is a separate decision for you. This round used your text **as written**, so the new section says "Capri Stone." If you'd like the entire site (footer, tab title, etc.) renamed to match, that's a one-round change we can do whenever you're ready.

## What did NOT change

- The hero section above is untouched.
- The collections preview, the walk-ins-welcome CTA, and the contact card below are all untouched — they just slide down to make room for the new section.
- Every other page on the site is unchanged.
